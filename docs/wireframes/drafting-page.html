MacBook Pro vs Dell XPS 15: Complete Comparison (2025)

Core Topic · Laptop Reviews 2025 · ~3,400 words · Last updated: 2 min ago

Performance Comparison

The performance gap between Apple's M3 Max and Intel's 14th-gen Core i9 processors tells an interesting story in 2025. While raw multi-threaded performance numbers favor Intel's 24-core architecture in sustained workloads, Apple's unified memory architecture delivers consistently faster results in real-world creative applications.

CPU Benchmarks: M3 Max vs Intel i9-14900H

In Cinebench R24, the i9-14900H scores approximately 1,820 in multi-core tests compared to the M3 Max's 1,650. However, the story changes dramatically when we look at power efficiency and thermal throttling behavior over extended workloads.

Benchmark MacBook Pro (M3 Max) Dell XPS 15 (i9-14900H)
Cinebench R24 (Single) 138 125
Cinebench R24 (Multi) 1,650 1,820
Geekbench 6 (Single) 3,150 2,890
Geekbench 6 (Multi) 21,400 16,800

GPU Performance for Creative Work

For video editors and 3D artists, GPU performance is often the decisive factor. The M3 Max's 40-core GPU with hardware-accelerated ray tracing delivers exceptional performance in Final Cut Pro and DaVinci Resolve, while the Dell XPS 15's NVIDIA RTX 4060 mobile GPU offers broader software compatibility.

Real-World Multitasking Tests

With 96GB of unified memory available on the MacBook Pro and up to 64GB of DDR5 on the Dell XPS, both machines handle demanding multitasking scenarios. Our test workflow included running a 4K video export while compiling code and browsing with 30+ Chrome tabs open simultaneously.
