**Macro-Semantics** operates at the structural and document-architecture level. While Micro-Semantics handles the granular word-by-word relationships (sentences), **Macro-Semantics** defines the overall **Contextual Vector**, the hierarchy of information, and how the document relates to the website's **Source Context**. It dictates the "skeleton" and "flow" of the meaning to ensure the search engine understands the primary focus immediately.

Here is the complete, actionable breakdown of Macro-Semantics optimization.

---

### **I. The Contextual Vector (The Straight Line of Meaning)**

The Contextual Vector is the semantic direction of the article. It must form a "straight line" from the H1 (Title) to the final heading. Deviating from this vector dilutes the document's relevance.

Every heading must be a natural successor to the previous one, staying within the boundaries of the **Macro Context**. | **Topic:** "Benefits of Drinking Water"H1: Benefits of Drinking WaterH2: Physical BenefitsH2: Psychological BenefitsH2: Nutritional Value (Zero calorie) | **Topic:** "Benefits of Drinking Water"H1: Benefits of Drinking WaterH2: History of Bottled WaterH2: Best Water Bottles to Buy (E-commerce intent breaking the informational flow) | ,, |
| **H1 Alignment** | The H1 must reflect the **Central Entity** and **Central Search Intent**. ### **III. The Macro vs. Micro Context Split (Page Segmentation)**

A semantic document is divided into two zones: **Main Content (Macro Context)** and **Supplementary Content (Micro Context)**. Managing the border between them is critical to prevent **Context Dilution**.

| Actie PUNT (Action Item) | REGEL (Rule Detail / Principle) | Goed Voorbeeld (Correct Application) | Fout Voorbeeld (Wrong Application) | Bronnen |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Establish the Border** | Use a specific heading or question to signal the transition from the main topic to related (but distinct) sub-topics. | **Article:** "Water Intoxication"Main Content ends.**Bridge Heading:** "What is the opposite of Water Intoxication?"Supplementary Content: "Dehydration". | Mixing paragraphs about "Dehydration" randomly inside the "Water Intoxication" section, confusing the main entity. | ,, |
| **Contextual Bridge** | Use a "Contextual Bridge" to connect the Macro Context to the Micro Context logically. | **Bridge:** "How does **electrolyte loss** relate to water intoxication?" (Connects the main topic 'intoxication' to the micro topic 'electrolytes'). | Jumping from "Water Intoxication" to "Best Sports Drinks" without explaining the connection (electrolytes). | , |
| **Supplementary Anchor Texts** | Place internal links to related but distinct topics in the **Supplementary Content** area (bottom of the page), not the Main Content, to avoid diluting the Macro Context early on. | Links to "Dehydration Symptoms" or "Water Filters" are placed at the bottom of the "Water Intoxication" article. | Placing a link to "Water Filters" in the first paragraph of "Water Intoxication", signaling the page might be about filters. | , |

### **V. Summary and Introduction Structure**

The introduction (first 400 characters) is the **Centerpiece Annotation**. It must summarize the Macro Context immediately.

* **Abstractive & Extractive Summary:** The introduction should function as a summary of the entire article. If you extracted the first sentence of every H2, they should form a coherent summary that matches the introduction.  
  * *Action:* Write the introduction *after* finishing the article to ensure it perfectly abstracts the content that follows,.  
* **Direct Definition:** Start with "X is Y." The very first sentence must define the Central Entity of the page.  
  * *Correct:* "Water intoxication is a medical condition caused by..."  
  * *Wrong:* "Have you ever wondered if drinking too much water is bad? In this article..." (Fluff delays the macro context signal),.

### **VI. Validation Checklist for Macro-Semantics**

1. **Does the H1 contain the Central Entity and the main attribute?**  
2. **Is the Contextual Vector linear?** (Do headings follow a logical "Definition \-\> attribute \-\> detail" order?)  
3. **Is there a clear border between Main and Supplementary content?**  
4. **Are internal links to different topics pushed to the Supplementary section?**  
5. **Does the content serve the Source Context (monetization purpose)?**  
6. **Is the answer to the main query located in the first 400 characters?**
